What is Tithi?

A Tithi is a lunar day in the Hindu calendar, calculated based on the moon's angular relationship with the sun. Each tithi occurs when the moon moves exactly 12 degrees away from the sun.

All 15 Tithis:
  • 1. Prathama (1st)
  • 2. Dwitiya (2nd)
  • 3. Tritiya (3rd)
  • 4. Chaturthi (4th)
  • 5. Panchami (5th)
  • 6. Shashthi (6th)
  • 7. Saptami (7th)
  • 8. Ashtami (8th)
  • 9. Navami (9th)
  • 10. Dashami (10th)
  • 11. Ekadashi (11th)
  • 12. Dwadashi (12th)
  • 13. Trayodashi (13th)
  • 14. Chaturdashi (14th)
  • 15. Purnima/Amavasya

Fun fact: Tithis don't have fixed durations - they can last anywhere from 19 to 26 hours!

What is Paksha?

Paksha refers to a fortnight or half-month in the Hindu lunar calendar, each consisting of 15 tithis.

Shukla Paksha (Waxing Moon):
15 days of growing moon energy, from new moon to full moon. This is considered auspicious for starting new ventures, marriages, and positive activities.
Krishna Paksha (Waning Moon):
15 days of decreasing moon energy, from full moon to new moon. This period is ideal for introspection, spiritual practices, and ancestor worship.

Associated Festivals:

  • Shukla Paksha: Diwali, Holi, Ganesh Chaturthi
  • Krishna Paksha: Shivaratri, Kali Puja, Pitru Paksha

What is Muhurat?

Muhurat is an auspicious time period, typically lasting 48 minutes, determined by calculating the most favorable planetary positions for specific activities.

5 Factors for Muhurat:
  • Tithi: Lunar day
  • Vara: Day of the week
  • Nakshatra: Constellation
  • Yoga: Angular relationship
  • Karana: Half tithi

Common Muhurat Types:

  • Vivah Muhurat (Marriage)
  • Griha Pravesh (House Entry)
  • Business Opening
  • Vehicle Purchase

Daily Muhurats: Brahma Muhurat (4-6 AM), Abhijit Muhurat (Noon), Godhuli Muhurat (Sunset)

Hindu Calendar Months

The Hindu lunar calendar consists of 12 months, each beginning with the new moon (Amavasya) or full moon (Purnima), depending on regional traditions.

12 Hindu Months:
  • Chaitra (Mar-Apr) - Spring
  • Vaishakha (Apr-May) - Spring
  • Jyeshtha (May-Jun) - Summer
  • Ashadha (Jun-Jul) - Summer
  • Shravana (Jul-Aug) - Monsoon
  • Bhadrapada (Aug-Sep) - Monsoon
  • Ashwin (Sep-Oct) - Autumn
  • Kartik (Oct-Nov) - Autumn
  • Margashirsha (Nov-Dec) - Pre-winter
  • Pausha (Dec-Jan) - Pre-winter
  • Magha (Jan-Feb) - Winter
  • Phalguna (Feb-Mar) - Winter

Festival Examples:

  • Chaitra: Rama Navami, Hanuman Jayanti
  • Kartik: Diwali, Karva Chauth
  • Phalguna: Holi, Shivaratri

Fun fact: Every 3 years, an extra month called "Adhik Maas" is added to align the lunar calendar with solar seasons!
