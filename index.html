      📚 Learn About Hindu Calendar

      🌙 What is Tithi?

      Tithi is a lunar day in the Hindu calendar. Unlike solar days (24 hours), tithis vary from 19-26 hours based on the moon's position.

      There are 30 tithis in a lunar month:

      • Shukla Paksha (Bright fortnight): 15 days when moon grows
      • Krishna Paksha (Dark fortnight): 15 days when moon shrinks

      The 15 Tithis:

      1. Prathama (Padyami)
      2. Dvitiya
      3. Tritiya
      4. Chaturthi
      5. Panchami
      6. Shashthi
      7. Saptami
      8. Ashtami
      9. Navami
      10. Dashami
      11. Ekadashi
      12. Dvadashi
      13. Trayodashi
      14. Chaturdashi
      15. Purnima (Full Moon) or Amavasya (New Moon)

      🕐 What is Muhurat?

      Muhurat is an auspicious time period of approximately 48 minutes (1/30th of a day).

      Why it matters: Ancient science of selecting favorable timing for important life events.

      Five Elements of Panchang:

      • 📅 Tithi - Lunar day
      • 📆 Vara - Weekday
      • ⭐ Nakshatra - Lunar mansion/constellation
      • 🔄 Yoga - Luni-solar day
      • ⚡ Karana - Half of tithi

      When Muhurat is Used:

      • 💍 Weddings and engagements
      • 🏢 Starting a new business
      • 🏠 Housewarming ceremony
      • 🚗 Buying vehicle or property
      • 📚 Starting education
      • 💼 Job interviews and exams
      • ✈️ Important travel
