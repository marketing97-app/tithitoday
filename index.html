What is Panchang? 📅

Simple Explanation

Panchang (meaning 'five limbs') is the Hindu calendar system that tracks lunar days, constellations, and auspicious timings. It's like a cosmic GPS for planning important life events!

The Five Elements

  • Tithi - Lunar day (like Monday, Tuesday but based on moon)
  • Vara - Weekday (Sunday to Saturday)
  • Nakshatra - Constellation/Star position
  • Yoga - Auspicious period
  • Karana - Half of a tithi

What is Tithi? 🌙

Simple Definition

A Tithi is a lunar day in the Hindu calendar. Unlike our regular calendar that follows the sun, Hindu festivals follow the moon's cycle!

Each tithi = Moon moving 12 degrees away from the sun

Two Phases

🌒 Shukla Paksha - Waxing moon (15 days)
Moon getting brighter

🌘 Krishna Paksha - Waning moon (15 days)
Moon getting darker

What is Muhurta? ⏰

Simple Definition

Muhurta is an auspicious time period of approximately 48 minutes. Think of it as finding the perfect time for important life events!

Why 48 minutes? It's based on ancient astronomical calculations that determine when planetary positions are most favorable.

When Do We Use Muhurta? 🤔

  • 💒 Weddings & engagements
  • 🏢 Starting new business
  • 🏠 Housewarming (Griha Pravesh)
  • 🚗 Buying vehicle or property
  • 📚 Starting education/exams
  • 💼 Job interviews
  • ✈️ Important travels
